Naming conventions

Two naming schemes are in common use. The meme.net.au site (Glenn McIntosh) labels pillars A through H by fiducial spacing. The dkrypt.org site (Greg Lloyd) numbers them 1 through 8. This site always shows both: Pillar A (#7). The mapping is: A=7, B=4, C=6, D=2, E=8, F=3, G=5, H=1.

Physical Structure

Heights, fiducials, and the pillar constellation.

Pillar heights

All ring ciphers sit at exactly 1709 mm above ground (uniform across all 8 pillars). The height variation between pillars is encoded as the extension above the ring. Pillar A (shortest) has zero extension; Pillar H (tallest) has 860 mm of extension, occupied by the PVL cipher.

All three series (total height, extension, fiducial separation) are monotone in pillar letter (A smallest, H largest). They are not the same measurement and must not be conflated.

Constellation layout

The eight pillars are arranged in a constellation pattern when viewed from above. Kohlhagen confirmed (2022, via Schmeh/Dunin) that positions and heights encode meaning. Working hypothesis: pillars B, G, F, C, D form the Southern Cross (Crux); pillars A, E, H represent Alpha, Beta, and Omega Centauri. The thematic link: Centaurus/Centenary wordplay for Canberra's 1913-2013 centenary.

Ring Cipher System

The labyrinth traversal pattern shared by all eight pillars.

All 8 pillars carry a 26x5 letter/symbol grid at the same height (1709mm). Read in labyrinth (boustrophedon) pattern. The four transition positions between row pairs are recorded as a 4-letter traversal code per pillar. These codes are believed to encode rotor settings or parameters for the Pillar C rotor machine.

Squircles Unsolved

10x26 grid of 4-orientation petal/cam shapes (encoded 0-3)

10x26 grid of 4-orientation petal/cam shapes (encoded 0-3). Creator hint (Kohlhagen via Schmeh): vertical pairs form a 26x5 labyrinth grid; 4 squircles per plaintext letter (codon-like redundancy); approximately 65 letters of plaintext. Best reading config: pair-boustrophedon with odd start column gives 50 distinct 4-cell codons. Pattern (1,3,0,3) appears 8 times (12.3%), matching English E frequency (12.7%).

Approaches tested
All simple substitutions, Vigenere, Bifid, Hill, autokey, running-key with solved poems, SA on homophonic - all rejected. 44 of 65 positions are singletons = too many free variables for frequency attack.

Pillar Pairings

Structural pairs derived from the Kohlhagen template.

The Kohlhagen template (shared with Schmeh and Dunin in 2022) reveals that ring ciphers sit at the same height across all pillars. Height differences encode the extension above the ring. Lower ciphers cluster into pairs by effective extent: G and F, E and D, C and A, H and B.

Implication: cross-pillar attacks should target pairs first. A solved pillar may unlock its partner. The template sharing was itself a deliberate hint that pillars should be read in relation to each other.

Relationship: Tightest pair, same group on both upper and lower channels

Key properties

Key format: 4-letter string setting offsets for rotors 1-4
Involutive: Yes (encrypt = decrypt with same key, no advancement)
Advancement: Manual (encoder chooses stepping sequence; no automatic odometer)

hdxjnfjxzjezthdbmmwqzjturogoucrfruohhzmlqpmbkuykkcrnkdlndlxjnidihjihqkwdpci

Partial solution

Positions 59-74 (last 16 chars) decrypt to thecentenarycode using rotor key SWYQ.

Unsolved: First 59 characters.
